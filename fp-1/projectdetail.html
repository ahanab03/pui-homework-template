Problem Space

Immigrants face tough job market hurdles. Stats show they're 2.6 times more likely to be unemployed than US-born individuals. Immigrant income as of 2023 is 20-25% lower than their US-born counterparts, underscoring a significant wage gap.

In response, I developed ProLinked, an app connecting immigrants with mentors for tailored job market guidance.

Project Goals

01

Easy to Navigate: Ensure that users can progress within the user flow with ease.

02

Understandable: Make sure icons and labels are clear for non-English speakers to understand.

03

Simple: Eliminate clutter and complexity wherever possible.

Interviews

User Personas

From our user research findings, I was able to create 2 user personas to explore and identify ProLinked’s main target users.

Goals

Get a good job in high tech

Pursue higher degrees from credible American schools

Has a plan and is determined to achieve it

Pains

Overwhelming competition in the job market

Challenges in obtaining higher education due to financial constraints

Lack of access to resources and networks

Goals

Find a stable job to support family

Improve English language skills for better job prospects

Transition smoothly into the American job market

Pains

Language barriers hinder communication with potential employers

Lack of understanding of the American job market dynamics

Difficulty in networking due to cultural differences

Homepage Mockup

Color: Blue

  • Represents calmness and stability.
  • Want to be reassuring towards new users.
  • Be a consistent and reliable app for users who hope to develop their skills.

Bottom Tab Bar

  • 49% of the time customers will use right thumb to navigate mobile apps, making a bottom nav bar more reachable.
  • Encourages scrolling.
  • Keeps the customer curious to explore all features as opposed to sandwich menu.

Browse Mentors

  • On home screen because it is the most important feature.
  • Primary button to draw the attention of users.

Icons

  • 47% of US immigrants are not proficient in English.
  • Making use of icons to provide information in a universal manner.
  • Easily understood by all ages.

Horizontal Scrolling

  • Gives most amount of information.
  • Provides user with a choice of what to explore.
  • Changes to vertical scrolling based on which category is explored.
